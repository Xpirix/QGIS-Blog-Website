<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Dear community,</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Due to some rather&nbsp;severe issues in the 3.16.13 and .12 Windows MSI installers, we decided to <a href="https://github.com/qgis/QGIS-Website/commit/2ef89726a066d9e4374bb210596500c228a6056d#diff-1618df4ceb52cef1cf94bb09e49c21d7ca0ff4281ee6b4021172872dfeb79969">temporarily&nbsp;revert back</a> the available download to the latest release without those issues, 3.16.11. The website rebuild has been performed and you&#8217;ll see everywhere that 3.16.11 is the latest LTR. This is true for Windows only as other&nbsp;OS will keep delivering the latest 3.16.13.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Next Friday 19th November is the planned release date for 3.16.14 which should bring fixes to both the above mentioned&nbsp;issues and restore the normal release flow.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Quoting our release manager Jürgen Fischer:&#8221;Only the 3.16.13 MSI is broken (not sure if 3.16.12-2 is affected).&nbsp;OSGeo4W<br>v2 was meanwhile fixed.&nbsp;All other platforms are not affected at all. The next release is on Friday and will also produce a fresh MSI.&#8221;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We apologize for the inconvenience and would like to take the opportunity to remind you how much work goes into producing and maintaining the high quality product that you&#8217;ve grown to love and that this is only possible thanks to our sustaining members and volunteers.&nbsp;If you or your organisation is relying&nbsp;on QGIS, it might be a good time to consider joining QGIS&#8217; funding effort at&nbsp;<a href="https://qgis.org/funding">https://qgis.org/funding</a> or&nbsp;<a href="https://github.com/sponsors/qgis/">https://github.com/sponsors/qgis/</a></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Have a great week, cheers</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Marco</p>
