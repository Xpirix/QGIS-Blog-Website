<p>Greetings wonderful QGIS Users, Developers and Supporters! 2016 was quite an  exciting year for QGIS &#8211; we released QGIS 2.14LTR which has been a great stable release. We also set the wheels in motion for QGIS 3.0 which we plan to release later this year. QGIS development takes a lot of time and effort &#8211; and much of the work is done by volunteers &#8211; especially the core project maintenance work which typically does not attract paid-for consulting work like new feature work does.</p>
<p>We are extremely lucky and grateful to have a growing band of sponsors that are helping us to slowly but surely fund more core QGIS project work too. In this post we want to say &#8216;thank you&#8217; to the many sponsors that have stepped up to the plate when we asked for help and sponsored QGIS. We also want to provide some insight as to what level of funding we receive and hopefully stimulate some friendly enthusiasm to stepping up funding efforts from our users around the world.</p>
<p>Our project treasurer, Andreas Neumann, has been so kind as to compile a very detailed report about the sponsorships and donations received over 2016 which we would like to share with you. Before I share his actual report, let me just clarify that we have two distinct ways of <a href="https://www.qgis.org/en/site/getinvolved/governance/sponsorship/sponsorship.html#qgis-sponsorship-program">contributing</a> money to the project:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Donations</strong>: You simply nominate an amount you wish to contribute and pay it to the project. Donations are intended as a way for individual users to show their support for QGIS (although we welcome large donations too).</li>
<li><strong>Sponsorships</strong>: Sponsorships are aimed at organizations. We have fixed tiers of contribution (see below) and each tier includes some benefits in terms of marketing exposure for your company or organisation.</li>
</ul>

<p>We had  <span style="font-size:2em;">1128</span> donations in 2016 amounting to a total of  <span style="font-size:2em;">€25108</span>. We had  <span style="font-size:2em;">47</span> Sponsors in 2016 which raised a total income of  <span style="font-size:2em;">€67921</span>. December is the month that attracts the highest income from donations and sponsors (though that may coincide with <a href="http://blog.qgis.org/2016/11/19/lets-make-a-big-funding-push-for-qgis-3-0/">additional marketing</a> we did calling for sponsors).  Looking at contributions by country, <span style="font-size:2em;">Denmark</span> was the highest national contributor in 2016, providing a total of  <span style="font-size:2em;">€17795</span> to the QGIS.ORG project. If you normalize by population,  <span style="font-size:2em;">Iceland</span> provided the largest per capita contribution to QGIS, providing  <span style="font-size:2em;">€9056 </span>per million people.  <span style="font-size:2em;">Iceland</span> also had the highest contribution rate when measured against GDP with  <span style="font-size:2em;">~€154</span> per billion GDP.</p>
