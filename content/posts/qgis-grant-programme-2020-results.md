<p>We are extremely pleased to announce the winning proposals for our 2020 QGIS.ORG grant programme. Funding for the programme was sourced by you, our <a href="https://www.qgis.org/en/site/about/sponsorship.html">project donors and sponsors</a>! <strong>Note:</strong> For more context surrounding our grant programme, please see: <a href="http://blog.qgis.org/2020/04/26/qgis-grants-5-call-for-grant-proposals-2020/">QGIS Grants #5: Call for Grant Proposals 2020</a>.</p>
<p>The QGIS.ORG Grant Programme aims to support work from our community that would typically not be funded by client/contractor agreements. This means that we did not accept proposals for the development of new features. Instead proposals focus on infrastructure improvements and polishing of existing features.<span id="more-1420"></span></p>
<p>Two proposals focusing on documentation improvements were <a href="https://www.qgis.org/en/site/getinvolved/governance/resolutions/2020_resolutions.html#fund-documentation-grant-proposals-from-documentation-budget">funded directly from the documentation budget</a>. The remaining 10 proposals continued on to the voting.</p>
<p>Voting to select the successful projects was carried out by our QGIS Voting Members. Each voting member was allowed to select up to 6 proposals. <p>Thanks to the generous support by our sponsors and donors, we are happy that all proposals will receive funding, even if QEP#124 had to be reduced in scope (core part only, no GUI: €2,<span style="font-weight:400;">600 from QGIS grants &amp; €1,400 sponsored by OPENGIS).</span></p>
<p>A couple of extra notes about the voting process:</p>
<ul>
<li>Voting was carried out based on the technical merits of the proposals and the competency of the applicants to execute on these proposals.</li>
<li>No restrictions were in place in terms of how many proposals could be submitted per person / organization, or how many proposals could be awarded to each proposing person / organization.</li>
<li>Voting was ‘blind’ (voters could not see the existing votes that had been placed).</li>
</ul>
<p>We received 34 votes from 21 community representatives and 13 user group representatives.</p>
<p>On behalf of the QGIS.ORG project, I would like to thank everyone who submitted proposals for this call!</p>
