<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Dear QGIS Community,</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We are very pleased to announce that this year’s round of grants is now available. The call is open to anybody who wants to make a contribution to QGIS funded by our grant fund, subject to the call conditions outlined in the application form.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This year&#8217;s budget is <strong>€50k</strong> and the deadline for the proposals is in four weeks, on Wednesday, <strong>26 March 2025</strong>. Here&#8217;s the full timeline: </p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Timeline</h2>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>2025-02-23: Call for proposals (4 weeks)</li>



<li>2025-03-26: QEP discussion period (2 weeks)</li>



<li>2025-04-09: Writing discussion summaries (1 week)</li>



<li>2025-04-16: Voting starts (2 weeks)</li>



<li>2025-04-30: Publication of results</li>



<li>&#8212; 6 months of project work &#8212;</li>



<li>2025-10-30: Deadline for follow-up reports</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Procedures</h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><span style="font-weight: 400">There are no new procedures in 2025. Please note the following guidelines established in previous years: </span></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><span style="font-weight: 400">The proposal must be submitted as a ‘QEP’ (QGIS Enhancement Proposal) issue in the repo: </span><a href="https://github.com/qgis/QGIS-Enhancement-Proposals"><span style="font-weight: 400">https://github.com/qgis/QGIS-Enhancement-Proposals</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> (tagged as Grant-2025). Following this approach will allow people to ask questions and provide public feedback on individual proposals.</span></li>



<li><span style="font-weight: 400">Proposals must clearly define the expected final result so that we can properly assess if the goal of the proposal has been reached.</span></li>



<li><span style="font-weight: 400">The project budgets should account for PR reviewing expenses to ensure timely handling of the project-related PRs and avoid delays caused by relying on reviewer volunteer time. </span></li>



<li><span style="font-weight: 400">In the week after the QEP discussion period, the proposal authors are expected to write a short summary of the discussion that is suitable for use as a basis on which voting members make their decisions. </span></li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><span style="font-weight: 400">The PSC of QGIS.ORG will examine the proposals and has veto power in case a proposal does not follow guidelines or is not in line with project priorities. </span></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">For more details, please read the introduction provided in <a href="https://forms.gle/YVJABJDwma8NQDUN9"><strong>the application form</strong></a>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We look forward to seeing all your great ideas for improving QGIS!</p>
