<p><em>Where is QGIS being developed?</em></p>
<p>That is a questions my students often ask. Open Source is a strange and new ‘world’ for most of them. So I try to explain: QGIS is a software, developed and maintained from all over the world by developers who are employed by companies, self-employed or working for free…</p>
<p>Some of the work is paid for by QGIS and some by users and as written &#8211; some do it for free &#8211; yay!  The core developers meet two times a year for ‘<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hackathon">Hackfests’</a>  (do not confuse with ‘Hacking’).</p>
<p>Sometimes a Hackfest is combined with a user conference – where developers and users can meet, listen to presentations and discuss functionality.</p>
<p>In 2017, the first Hackfest will take place at the Linuxhotel in Essen – Germany from Friday from 28th April &#8211; 1st May. This Hackfest is only going to be hard work for the developers – QGIS 3.0 is being developed and launched this year. More details and signing in for this weekend on the <a href="https://github.com/qgis/QGIS/wiki/QGIS-Hackfest---Essen-April-2017">event wiki page</a>.</p>
<p>The second Hackfest in 2017 will include a Summer Camp and take place in <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wi1o9n4eX7o">Nødebo</a> at University of Copenhagen, Forest and Landscape College (Denmark) from Wednesday  2. August till  Friday 11. august</p>
<p>The Summer Camp will be a combination of work and leisure for the developers. And for users there will be workshops.</p>
<p>It is the first time we are having a Summer Camp at the Forest and Landscape College. We have both the place for work and the nature for exploring.</p>
<p>There are 28 rooms/56 beds, 3 large shelters and a large lawn where you can bring a tent sleeping bag and mattress.</p>
<p>Nearby the wonderful forest and lake.</p>
<p>The setup is as following:</p>
<p>Users pay for participating in workshops, food and accommodation (room/bed) – Shelter and tent are free.</p>
<p>Developers and workshop lecturers stays for free.</p>
<p>Call for workshops and sponsors: If you have a topic for a workshop or want to contribute as sponsor, please send me an e-mail at <a href="mailto:lfi@ign.ku.dk">lfi@ign.ku.dk</a></p>
<p>Save the dates – and we will send out more information about the Summer Camp later this month.</p>
