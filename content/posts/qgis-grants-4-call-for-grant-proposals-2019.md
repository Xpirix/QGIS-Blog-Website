<p>Dear QGIS Community</p>
<p>Our first three rounds of Grant Proposals were a great success. We are very pleased to announce the fourth round of grants is now available to QGIS contributors.</p>
<p>Based on <a href="http://blog.qgis.org/2019/01/07/user-question-of-the-month-jan19-answers-from-dec/">community feedback</a>, this year, we will not accept proposals for the development of new features. Instead proposals should focus on infrastructure improvements and polishing of existing features.</p>
<p>The deadline for this round is <strong>Sunday, 2 June 2019</strong>. All the details for the grants are described in the <a href="https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfXlSTr8l-ndUyLLv6BMqJYq7Ev7bJPipQw2o2UUZuqm9S3bg/viewform?usp=sf_link"><strong>application form</strong></a>, and for more context we encourage you to also read last year&#8217;s articles:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://blog.qgis.org/2018/04/15/qgis-grants-3-call-for-grant-proposals-2018/">QGIS Grants #3: Call for Grant Proposals 2018</a></li>
<li><a href="http://blog.qgis.org/2018/06/22/qgis-grant-programme-2018-results/">QGIS Grant Programme 2018 Results</a></li>
</ul>
<p>We look forward to seeing all your great ideas about how to improve QGIS!</p>
<p>Anita Graser</p>
<p>QGIS PSC</p>
