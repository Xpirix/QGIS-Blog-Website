<p></span></span>The Call for Papers and Workshops for the 2nd International QGIS User and Developer Conference, is <span style="font-family:Corbel;">still</span> open!</p>
<p><b>Call for Presentations</b><br />
Deadline: <b>February </b><b><span style="font-family:Corbel;">22nd</span></b></p>
<p>The QGIS Conference presentations are 20 minutes long, with time for Q&amp;A at the end of each talk. Presentations may cover any aspect related with the use or development of QGIS software. Anyone can can submit a presentation proposal and take part in the conference as a presenter. The received proposals will be reviewed by the program committee.</p>
<p><b>See already submitted presentations</b> and details<span style="font-family:Corbel;">:</span> <a class="moz-txt-link-freetext" href="http://www.sigte.udg.edu/jornadassiglibre/en/international-qgis-user-and-developer-conference/conferencia-qgis/">http://www.sigte.udg.edu/jornadassiglibre/en/international-qgis-user-and-developer-conference/conferencia-qgis/</a></p>
<p><b>Call for Workshops</b><br />
Deadline: <b>February </b><b><span style="font-family:Corbel;">22nd</span></b></p>
<p>There are two kinds of workshops depending on the duration: 2 hours or 4 hours. If you want to actively participate in the 2nd Int. QGIS Conference and impart a workshop, don&#8217;t hesitate to send your workshop proposal to <a class="moz-txt-link-abbreviated" href="mailto:talleres@sigte.org">talleres@sigte.org</a></p>
<p>The proposal should be a brief abstract pointing out the expected duration of the workshop (2 hours, 4 hours) as well as a few lines describing the content of the workshop, pre-requisites for the attendants (if needed), <span style="font-family:Corbel;">name of the <span style="font-family:Corbel;">instructo<span style="font-family:Corbel;">r</span></span></span>&#8230;</p>
<p>The instructors of the selected workshops will <span style="font-family:Corbel;">receive</span> a free pass for the conference.</p>
